<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="uses">What is oxycodone?</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.drugs.com/monograph/oxycodone.html">Oxycodone</a> is an opioid pain medication sometimes called a narcotic.</p>
<p>Oxycodone is used to treat <a href="https://www.drugs.com/cg/pain-management.html">moderate to severe pain</a>.</p>
<p>The <b>extended-release form</b> of oxycodone is for around-the-clock treatment of pain and should <b>not</b> be used on an as-needed basis for pain.</p>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="warnings">Warnings</h2>
<p>You should not use oxycodone if you have severe <a href="https://www.drugs.com/cg/asthma.html" onclick="ga('send', 'event', 'Content Link', 'asthma');">asthma</a> or breathing problems, or a blockage in your stomach or intestines.</p>
<p><b>MISUSE OF OPIOID MEDICINE CAN CAUSE ADDICTION, OVERDOSE, OR DEATH.</b> Keep the medication in a place where others cannot get to it.</p>
<p><b>Taking oxycodone during pregnancy may cause life-threatening withdrawal symptoms in the newborn.</b></p>
<p><b>Fatal side effects can occur if you use opioid medicine with alcohol, or with other drugs that cause drowsiness or slow your breathing.</b></p>
<p><b>Oxycodone can slow or stop your breathing.</b> This is more likely in elderly or ill patients, but can occur in anyone taking this medicine.</p>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="before-taking">Before taking this medicine</h2>
<p>You should not use oxycodone if you are allergic to it, or if you have:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>severe <a href="/cg/asthma.html">asthma</a> or breathing problems; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>a blockage in your stomach or intestines.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>You should not use this medicine if you are already using a similar opioid medicine and are tolerant to it. Do not use this medicine if you have used a MAO inhibitor in the past 14 days, such as isocarboxazid, linezolid, phenelzine, rasagiline, selegiline, or tranylcypromine or have received a methylene blue injection.</p>
<p>Most brands of oxycodone are not approved for use in people under the age of 18. <b>OxyContin</b> should not be given to a child younger than 11 years old.</p>
<p>To make sure this medicine is safe for you, tell your doctor if you have ever had:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>breathing problems, sleep apnea;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>a <a href="/cg/head-injury.html">head injury</a>, brain tumor, or <a href="/cg/frontal-lobe-seizures.html">seizures</a>;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>drug or alcohol addiction, or mental illness;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>lung disease;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>liver or kidney disease;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>thyroid disorder;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>adrenal disease (such as Addison's disease;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>urination problems; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>problems with your gallbladder or pancreas.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>If you use opioid medicine while you are pregnant, your baby could become dependent on the drug.</b> This can cause life-threatening withdrawal symptoms in the baby after it is born. Babies born dependent on <a href="/drug-class/narcotic-analgesics.html">opioids</a> may need medical treatment for several weeks. Tell your doctor if you are pregnant before using oxycodone. If you become pregnant while taking oxycodone, do not stop your medication suddenly without talking to your doctor. You may need to decrease your medicine gradually.</p>
<p><b>Ask a doctor before using oxycodone if you are breastfeeding.</b> Tell your doctor if you notice severe drowsiness or slow breathing in the nursing baby.</p>

<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="dosage">How should I use oxycodone?</h2>
<p>Take oxycodone exactly as prescribed. Follow the directions on your prescription label and read all medication guides. Never use this medicine in larger amounts, or for longer than prescribed. Tell your doctor if you feel an increased urge to take more of oxycodone.</p>
<p><b>Never share opioid medicine with another person, especially someone with a history of drug abuse or addiction. MISUSE CAN CAUSE ADDICTION, OVERDOSE, OR DEATH.</b> Keep the medication in a place where others cannot get to it. Selling or giving away opioid medicine is against the law.</p>
<p>Stop taking all other around-the-clock opioid pain medicines when you start taking extended-release oxycodone.</p>
<p>Take with food.</p>
<p><b>Swallow the capsule or tablet whole</b> to avoid exposure to a potentially fatal overdose. Do not crush, chew, break, open, or dissolve.</p>
<p><b>If you cannot swallow a capsule whole,</b> open it and sprinkle the medicine into a spoonful of pudding or applesauce. Swallow the mixture right away without chewing. Do not save it for later use.</p>
<p>Never crush or break an oxycodone pill to inhale the powder or mix it into a liquid to inject the drug into your vein. This can cause in death.</p>
<p>Measure liquid medicine carefully. Use the dosing syringe provided, or use a medicine dose-measuring device (not a kitchen spoon).</p>
<p>You should not stop using oxycodone suddenly. Follow your doctor's instructions about gradually decreasing your dose.</p>
<p>Store at room temperature, away from heat, moisture, and light. Keep track of your medicine. Oxycodone is a drug of abuse and you should be aware if anyone is using your medicine improperly or without a prescription.</p>
<p>Do not keep leftover opioid medication. <b>Just one dose can cause death in someone using this medicine accidentally or improperly.</b> Ask your pharmacist where to locate a drug take-back disposal program. If there is no take-back program, flush the unused medicine down the toilet.</p>
<div class="ddc-related-link"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="ddc-icon ddc-icon-info-filled" focusable="false" height="20" viewbox="0 0 24 24" width="20" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg"><path d="M12 1.5a10.5 10.5 0 1 0 0 21 10.5 10.5 0 0 0 0-21ZM12 6a1.125 1.125 0 1 1 0 2.25A1.125 1.125 0 0 1 12 6Zm3 12.094H9v-1.688h2.156v-4.312H9.75v-1.688h3.094v6H15v1.688Z"></path></svg> <span><a data-action="drug-dosage-disambiguation" data-brand_id="0" data-ddc_id="1770" href="/dosage/oxycodone.html">Detailed Oxycodone dosage information</a> </span></div>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="missed-dose">What happens if I miss a dose?</h2>
<p>Since oxycodone is used for pain, you are not likely to miss a dose. Skip any missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not use two doses at one time.</p>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="overdose">What happens if I overdose?</h2>
<p>Seek emergency medical attention or call the Poison Help line at 1-800-222-1222. <b>An oxycodone overdose can be fatal, especially in a child or other person using the medicine without a prescription.</b> Overdose symptoms may include severe drowsiness, pinpoint pupils, slow breathing, or no breathing.</p>
<p>Your doctor may recommend you get naloxone (a medicine to reverse an opioid overdose) and keep it with you at all times. A person caring for you can give the naloxone if you stop breathing or don't wake up. Your caregiver must still get emergency medical help and may need to perform CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation) on you while waiting for help to arrive.</p>
<p>Anyone can buy naloxone from a pharmacy or local health department. Make sure any person caring for you knows where you keep naloxone and how to use it.</p>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="what-to-avoid">What should I avoid while using oxycodone?</h2>
<p><b>Do not drink alcohol.</b> Dangerous side effects or death could occur.</p>
<p>Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this medicine will affect you. <a href="/cg/vertigo.html">Dizziness</a> or severe drowsiness can cause falls or other accidents.</p>
<p>Avoid medication errors. Always check the brand and strength of oxycodone you get from the pharmacy.</p>
<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="side-effects">Oxycodone side effects</h2>
<p>Get emergency medical help if you have <b>signs of an allergic reaction to oxycodone:</b> <a href="/cg/urticaria.html">hives</a>; difficult breathing; swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat.</p>
<p>Opioid medicine, including oxycodone, can slow or stop your breathing, and death may occur. A person caring for you should give naloxone and/or seek emergency medical attention if you have slow breathing with long pauses, blue colored lips, or if you are hard to wake up.</p>
<p>Call your doctor at once if you have:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>noisy breathing, sighing, shallow breathing, breathing that stops during sleep;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>a <a href="/cg/bradycardia.html">slow heart rate</a> or weak pulse;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>cold, clammy skin;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>a light-headed feeling, like you might pass out;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>confusion, unusual thoughts or behavior;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>seizure (convulsions);</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><b>low cortisol levels</b> - nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, dizziness, worsening tiredness or weakness; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><b>high levels of serotonin in the body</b> - agitation, hallucinations, fever, sweating, shivering, fast heart rate, muscle stiffness, twitching, loss of coordination, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Serious breathing problems may be more likely in older adults and in those who are debilitated or have wasting syndrome or chronic breathing disorders.</b></p>
<p>Long-term use of opioid medication, such as oxycodone, may affect fertility (ability to have children) in men or women. It is not known whether opioid effects on fertility are permanent.</p>
<p>Common oxycodone side effects may include:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>drowsiness, dizziness, tiredness;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="/cg/acute-headache.html">headache</a>;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="/cg/constipation.html">constipation</a>, stomach pain, nausea, vomiting; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>itching, red eyes, or flushing.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>This is not a complete list of side effects and others may occur. Call your doctor for medical advice about side effects. You may report side effects to FDA at 1-800-FDA-1088.</p>

<h2 class="ddc-anchor-offset" id="interactions">What other drugs will affect oxycodone?</h2>
<p>You may have breathing problems or withdrawal symptoms if you start or stop taking certain other medicines. Tell your doctor if you also use an <a href="/article/antibiotics.html">antibiotic</a>, antifungal medication, heart or blood pressure medication, seizure medication, or medicine to treat <a href="/cg/hiv-infection.html">HIV</a> or <a href="/cg/hepatitis-c.html">hepatitis C</a>.</p>
<p><b>Opioid medication can interact with many other drugs and cause dangerous side effects or death.</b> Be sure your doctor knows if you also use:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><b>other opioids</b> - opioid pain medicine or prescription cough medicine;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><b>sedative medications</b> including alprazolam or Xanax, clonazepam or Klonopin, diazepam or Valium, lorazepam or Ativan, temazepam or Restoril and others;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>sleeping pills;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>muscle relaxants or tranquilizers;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>medicine for depression, anxiety, or other mental illness;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>medicine for Parkinson’s disease;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>migraine headache treatment; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>medications used for the prevention of nausea and vomiting.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>cold or allergy medicines, bronchodilator asthma/COPD medication, or a diuretic ("water pill");</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>medicines for motion sickness, irritable bowel syndrome, or overactive bladder;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>This list is not complete and many other drugs may interact with oxycodone. This includes prescription and over-the-counter medicines, <a href="/drug-class/vitamins.html">vitamins</a>, and <a href="/drug-class/herbal-products.html">herbal products</a>. Not all possible drug interactions are listed here.</p>

Oxycontin releases oxycodone slowly and continuously over 12 hours and only needs to be given twice a day. Oxycodone is shorter-acting, with an average half-life of 3.2 hours, which means that half the dose of oxycodone is eliminated in 3.2 hours, which is why it is usually given four to six times a day to provide all-day pain relief. Oxycodone is usually given for acute pain, such as that following surgery or trauma, whereas Oxycontin may be given for chronic or long-term pain, such as that caused by cancer. Oxycontin should only be considered in those with chronic severe pain that have already found a trial of oxycodone to be beneficial. <a class="ddc-text-nowrap ddc-link-forward" href="/medical-answers/oxycodone-vs-oxycontin-3039094/">Continue reading</a></p></div>
